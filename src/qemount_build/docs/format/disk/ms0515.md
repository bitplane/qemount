The Elektronika MS 0515 was a Soviet desktop microcomputer built around a
PDP-11-compatible processor, part of the broader family of Soviet PDP-11 clones.
This format is the raw sector-image dump of an MS 0515 floppy as read by MAME.

The disk is a 5.25-inch double-density MFM layout: 80 tracks, 10 sectors per
track and 512-byte sectors, recorded at 300 rpm with 2000 bit-cells per
revolution. MAME provides both a single-sided (1 head) and a double-sided (2
heads) variant. The MAME source notes that its gap sizes (50, 22, 30 bytes) were
taken from the machine's own `FORML.SAV` formatting program.

The image carries no header or magic signature — it is a bare fixed-geometry
sector dump — so no Detection section is given and no size-based rule is implied.

It belongs to the same Soviet PDP-11 lineage as the Elektronika BK and DVK
machines whose disk formats are catalogued separately.
