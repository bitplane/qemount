# Sord M5 floppy image

A raw sector image for floppies of the Sord M5, a compact Japanese Z80A home
computer launched by the Sord Computer Corporation in 1982 (also sold by Takara
as a games machine). Cartridges and cassettes were the primary media; floppy
support came later, including 3" disks via the FD-5 drive.

## Structure

MAME's loader defines two related, headerless geometries (raw sector dumps, no
magic number):

**`m5_format`** — 5.25" double-sided, double-density:

- 40 tracks, 2 heads
- 18 sectors per track, 256 bytes per sector (≈ 360 KB)
- MFM, 300 RPM; gap layout 80 / 50 / 22 / 80

**FD-5 format** (`.fd5`, `.img`) — 3" disks, MFM, in two sizes:

- Single-sided: 40 tracks × 1 head × 16 sectors × 256 bytes = 160 KB
- Double-sided: 40 tracks × 2 heads × 16 sectors × 256 bytes = 320 KB

All variants are flat, fixed-geometry sector images; identification is by size
and context rather than any header. The `.dsk` extension carries the 5.25"
layout, while `.fd5`/`.img` carry the 3" FD-5 layouts.
